Mae Jemison

The first African-American Woman to go to space


Here is a timeline of Dr. Jemison's life:

  • 1956-Born in Decatur, Alabama
  • 1959-Family moved to Chicago, Illinois to take advantage of a better educational & employment opprotunities
  • 1966-She began dancing
  • 1969-She auditioned for the leading role in West Side Story, but she didn't get the part.
  • 1973-Graduated from Chicago's Morgan Park High School and entered Stanford at 16
  • 1975-She was a member of the Black Students Union during her college years.
  • 1977-Graduated from Stanford with a B.S. in Chemical Engineering and also a B.A. in African & Afro-American Studies
  • 1981-Mae got her Doctor of Medicine degree from Cornell Medical College
  • 1983-She joined the Peace Corps and served as a Peace Corps Medical officer for 2+ years.
  • 1983-She applied for the astronaut program., but after the Challenger diaster, she had to wait.
  • 1987-Mae reappied and was accepted. She was 1 of 15 candidates choseing out of about 2,000 applicants.
  • 1992-She flew her only space mission from September 12th to the 20th as a Mission Specialist on the STS-47. Jemison logged 190 hours, 30 minutes, 23 seconds in space
